# RBI Fruits Working Paper — primary-source read (banana comb)

> **WP identity:** RBI Working Paper Series **WPS (DEPR): 06/2024**, *"Price Dynamics and Value Chain of Fruits in India: A Study of Grapes, Bananas and Mangoes"*, by **Raya Das, Ranjana Roy, Sanchit Gupta, Sanjib Bordoloi, Rishabh Kumar, Renjith Mohan, Ashok Gulati** (RBI–ICRIER joint project), **October 2024**. Sister paper to the TOP paper ([[PS4 - RBI TOP Decomposition]], WPS 08/2024 — same project, same method, overlapping authors).
> Read via pdftotext; page numbers below are the printed footer numbers. Banana combed fully; mango kept only where it is contractor case law (pp. 32–34).

## Headline (abstract, p.2)
Farmers' share in the consumer rupee: **banana 31%, grapes 35%, mango 43%** (domestic chains). ARDL finds a long-run inverse relationship between monthly availability-usage ratio and prices for all three fruits.

## Banana price dynamics (Section III.2, pp. 16–17)
- Retail inflation spiked to **18%** Sep–Dec 2013 (monsoon deficiency + area contraction 0.83→0.78 Mha).
- Shock catalogue: *banana skipper* pest, Karnataka 2015-16 → ~30% weight loss; **Panama TR4 outbreak 2017-18 → >10,000 ha infected**; Mar 2023 urban price spike from transport/storage costs + rain-hit supply (GJ/MH/AP).
- Water is the swing factor: banana is a water guzzler; depleted water tables push growers out of the crop.

## Banana domestic value chain (pp. 29–30) — THE core numbers
Case study: **Jalgaon (production) → Delhi (consumption)**, Agmarknet + NHB + field visit Jan 2023 (FGDs with Jalgaon Fruit Sale Cooperative, farmers, traders, two APMCs incl. Vashi, the perishables transport association, and **FPO Sahyadri**).

- **Farmers' share in consumer rupee: 30.8%** (Chart 29). **Ranges 20%–35%; rises to ~35% after peak-harvest.**
- Mark-ups at each stage = margin + cost incurred (gross, same convention as the TOP paper):
  - **Trader:** main cost = long-haul transport Jalgaon→Delhi.
  - **Wholesaler:** labour + **ripening** + local transport from mandi.
  - **Retailer:** perishability/wastage risk.
- Chart 29 is an image — exact stage-wise % not in extractable text. If we need the split, read the chart visually from the PDF (p. 30).

## Banana processing & waste (pp. 30–32)
- **~10% of fresh banana goes to processing** (MoFPI 2021): puree, concentrate, powder (baby-food demand), chips.
- Puree flow: harvest → ripening chamber (4 days, 18°C) → wash → peel/extract. Chips flow includes **grading** as an explicit step.
- Processing units lack vertical integration; no quality standardisation/branding → export chips market uncaptured (Gulati et al. 2022).
- **Pseudo-stem waste: 70–80 MT/ha** from stem removal → core (candies/pickles/drinks), fibre (currency paper, fabric), waste (bio-fertiliser/vermicompost). Round-the-year raw material because banana is perennial. Framed as a farmer-income lever (pp. 32, 54).

## Mango PHC — contractor case law (pp. 32–34; kept because it IS our contractor question)
- **Pre-Harvest Contractors (PHCs)** contract with farmers *before* harvest based on flowering: fix price + tonnage + **provide orchard-maintenance support**. Aggregation for economies of scale; small growers rely on them.
- Post-harvest: PHC → ripening chambers (max 7 days, room temp) → sold immediately (no storage — pulp quality) → commission agents/APMC at consumption centre → **sorting & grading done by commission agents** → retail.
- Malihabad→Delhi decomposition (Table 7): **farmer 42–43%** (₹62–67 of ₹149–155), PHC margin 10%, transport-to-ripening 5%, labour 2%, wholesale/commission 7%, **value loss (weight/grading/sorting/packaging) 8%**, retailer 27%.
- Read-across: the *highest* farmer share of the three fruits coexists WITH a contractor layer — because the PHC bundles price insurance + maintenance + aggregation, and the chain has low pre-wholesale losses. Supports [[02 - Research/PS4 Agri/The Spine - Central Argument|C5]] (unbundle, don't eliminate) against naive disintermediation.
- Export chain: exporters buy at market rate, bear all downstream costs; APEDA-coordinated treatments; **>50k farmers on Hortinet/MangoNet for traceability**.

## ARDL result for banana (Section VII, pp. 45–46)
- Long-run cointegration confirmed (Bounds test, 1%). **A 1% rise in the availability-usage ratio lowers banana CPI by ~0.21% in the long run** (ARDL 4,2; Jul 2012–Jun 2022; dummies for pest/rainfall shocks).
- This is the empirical warrant for supply-smoothing: coordinated arrivals (staggered planting, harvest-window management) measurably move retail price.

## Policy chapter (Section IX, pp. 52–55) — the paper's own prescription list
1. **Strengthening supply chain:** farmers sell immediately post-harvest due to *no storage + advance credit already taken* → low realisation; ~15-day sale window across fruits. Prescriptions: cold storage at both ends; **more ripening centres at consumption hubs** (banana-specific); dedicated fruit transport vehicles; packaging innovation; **B2B models + more FPOs**.
2. **Cultivars:** tissue culture + drip for banana (numbers above); extension for time-bound pesticide use.
3. **Crop insurance** for weather-driven volatility.
4. **Processing/export:** the pseudo-stem waste play; ~10% already processed (higher than other horticulture).
5. **Trade policy:** 30%+ import duty could be cut in lean seasons to cap price spikes.
6. **Digital traceability:** **"For banana, traceability is not widely followed; hence, the presence of Indian banana is miniscule in global trade."** GrapeNet/MangoNet exist and work (GAP enforcement, arrival forecasting from flowering/pruning data); **banana has NO equivalent net** — an explicit, RBI-stated gap that our platform's lot-identity layer fills.

## Deltas vs what the vault already believed
1. **C1 cross-validated on fruits:** farmer share 31–43% (fruits) brackets the TOP 33.5–36.7% (vegetables). Identical four-stakeholder gross-markup structure. The ₹100-waterfall logic carries to banana.
2. **Banana is the LOW end (30.8%)** — below tomato. The wholesaler's mark-up includes **ripening**, a stage vegetables don't have: an extra internalizable hop (GT Deck: ripening usage charge ₹2/kg in Theni).
3. **Mango PHC = strongest evidence yet that contractors are a bundle, not a toll** (highest farmer share WITH contractors). Kills any "remove the contractor" framing.
4. **"BananaNet doesn't exist"** — RBI names the traceability void our system fills. Citable warrant.
5. **Availability-usage elasticity (−0.21)** gives supply-smoothing an RBI-grade number.
