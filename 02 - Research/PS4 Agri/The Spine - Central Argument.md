# The Spine — the central argument as it evolves

> **Format law:** the argument is decomposed into numbered claims (C1…). Each claim carries: current wording, status, evidence links, its strongest challenger, and a **version history** — we never silently rewrite a claim; we version it. The current one-paragraph argument at top is regenerated whenever any claim changes.
> Status ladder: 🌱 hypothesis → ⚔️ contested → 🟢 settled → ⚰️ killed (kills stay visible — they're element-4 gold).

## The argument as of 2026-07-17 (v4)
The consumer's ₹100 decomposes (RBI WPS 08/2024, tomato): farmer ₹33.5, trader ₹21.3, wholesaler ₹16.1, retailer ₹29.1 — and the fruits sister paper (WPS 06/2024) confirms the same structure on our crop: **banana farmer share 30.8%**, the lowest of the fruits, with an extra internalizable hop vegetables don't have (**ripening**, sitting inside the wholesaler's mark-up). The middlemen *keep* only ~₹10 of their ~₹37 mark-up; the attackable objects are the **cost stack** and the **retail hop**. So replacing the actors is pointless — the mango PHC chain proves it (highest farmer share of the three fruits, 42–43%, WITH a contractor layer). The winning move is **e-Choupal's law: asset-light coordination over ownership** — instantiated as [[e-Choupal Banana OS]]: run the chain backwards from the retailer's demand schedule, grade at the farm gate, give every lot a digital identity (RBI itself says banana has no GrapeNet/MangoNet equivalent), allocate ripening by order, and run hub-and-spoke logistics with loaded backhauls. Finance stays on partner rails (day-0 cash is the real lock — RBI: farmers sell at harvest because advances are already taken). One crop (banana), one district (Theni), Sahyadri's sequencing. Supply-smoothing has an RBI number now: +1% availability-usage ratio → −0.21% banana CPI.

## Claims
### C1 — The prize is mis-located: it's the cost stack (~₹27) and the retail hop (~₹30), not any actor's profit pocket (~₹10)
- **Status:** 🟢 settled on numbers (RBI WPS 08/2024, page-cited); wording may still sharpen
- **Evidence:** Full decomposition ([[../PS4 - RBI TOP Decomposition]]): tomato = farmer 33.5 / trader 21.3 / wholesaler 16.1 / retailer 29.1 (p.22); onion 36.2/17.6/15.0/31.3 (p.25); potato 36.7/16.2/16.0/31.0 (p.27). Trader & wholesaler NET margins ~5.3% each (pp.21–22) — of their ~₹37 mark-up, ~₹27 is cost (transport, mandi fees, wastage), ~₹10 is kept profit. Storage losses p.33; onion 10→30% Jul–Dec p.35. **Cross-validated on fruits 07-17** ([[../PS4 - RBI Fruits Decomposition]], WPS 06/2024): banana 30.8% / grapes 35% / mango 42–43% — same four-stakeholder gross-markup convention; banana's wholesaler markup additionally contains ripening cost.
- **Strongest challenger (resolved):** Disintermediation school's "stacked margins" objection — the stack is real (₹37) but it's a *cost* stack, not a *rent* stack. Disintermediating the actors without deleting their costs re-assigns the ₹27 to the FPO's own P&L.
- **History:** v1 (07-09, imported prior): "middlemen capture the wedge — cut them out." → v2 (07-10): "trader takes only ~5%, the ₹56 block is retail+wastage" — *from secondary coverage.* → **v3 (07-10): primary source corrects v2 — retail is ₹29–31 not ₹56; middlemen mark up ₹37 but keep ~₹10; the attackable object is the ₹27 cost stack + the ₹30 retail hop.** *Trigger: Sonnet extraction of WPS 08/2024. Lesson logged: never let a residual masquerade as a finding (Quant rule 5 vindicated).*

### C3 — "Own" means own the order book + quality gate; rent everything physical
- **Status:** ⚔️ contested — the brief's own Exhibit F ("moving up the chain works better") is the counter-argument; Sahyadri eventually DID buy assets
- **Evidence:** Sahyadri sequence (contracts → assets, 15 yrs); Dell/Li&Fung coordination logic; deletion table in Interrogation Step 2
- **Strongest challenger:** Move-up-the-chain school — value addition is where realized returns rose (brief, Exhibit F)
- **Resolution path:** reconcile via *sequencing*, not either/or: order-book-first IS the year-0–2 answer; assets are the year-3+ answer once volume de-risks them. Needs Sahyadri early-years financials (Ledger queue #6) to settle.
- **History:** v1 (07-10): "asset-light forever" → v2 (07-10): softened to sequencing after Sahyadri precedent pull showed assets were how they scaled margin. *Trigger: Anchor Buyer/Exemplar persona sourcing.*

### C5 — Unbundle the arthiya, don't eliminate him: match day-0 cash, undercut 15–24% credit, add what opacity can't copy (grading, traceability)
- **Status:** 🌱 hypothesis, thesis-shaping — candidate for THE one-sentence recommendation
- **Evidence:** IGC/CASI interlinked-transaction literature; credit share ≥35% via agents; his trading margin thin (C1). **New 07-17:** mango PHC decomposition ([[../PS4 - RBI Fruits Decomposition]] pp.32–34) — pre-harvest contractors bundle price-fixing + orchard-maintenance credit + aggregation, and the chain still delivers the *highest* farmer share (42–43%) of the three fruits. The contractor is a bundle, not a toll; unbundling beats elimination on primary evidence now.
- **Strongest challenger:** Credit-first school pushed to its limit says the FPO should *become* the lender — but NBFC-ization is a capability/regulatory trap (Adversary: CRASH axis). Current wording deliberately stops at "partner rails" (KCC/pledge/anchor), not lending off own book.
- **History:** v1 (07-10): born from Arthiya persona sourcing. *Trigger: "his profit is the credit book, not the trade."*

### C6 — One crop, one district, demand-first: **banana, Theni** (the Sahyadri sequencing)
- **Status:** ⚔️ contested — crop/district now chosen by direction (Charan, 07-17), not yet by sweet-spot screening; screening (Ledger queue #4) becomes the *validation* pass rather than the selector
- **Evidence:** Sahyadri origin (one export grape, Nashik); brief's punishment of optionality. Banana case: perennial year-round supply in TN (no seasonal cliff), farmer share at the LOW end (30.8% — most headroom), an extra internalizable hop (ripening), an RBI-named traceability void (no BananaNet), and a district dataset already in hand ([[GT Deck]]: 5 FPOs in Theni, channel shares, ₹2/kg ripening, ₹1–1.5/kg transport)
- **Strongest challenger:** the un-run sweet-spot screen — if onion/pomegranate/chilli beat banana on TAM × buyer concentration × perishability fit, direction must yield to numbers (README precedence rule)
- **History:** v1 (07-10): imported from PS2's one-product discipline, crop open. → **v2 (07-17): banana/Theni adopted as the working instantiation; e-Choupal centered as the operating template.** *Trigger: Charan's e-Choupal working notes + GT Cluster-2 deck + RBI WPS 06/2024 assimilation → [[e-Choupal Banana OS]].*
