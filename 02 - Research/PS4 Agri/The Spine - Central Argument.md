# The Spine — the central argument as it evolves

> **Format law:** the argument is decomposed into numbered claims (C1…). Each claim carries: current wording, status, evidence links, its strongest challenger, and a **version history** — we never silently rewrite a claim; we version it. The current one-paragraph argument at top is regenerated whenever any claim changes.
> Status ladder: 🌱 hypothesis → ⚔️ contested → 🟢 settled → ⚰️ killed (kills stay visible — they're element-4 gold).

## The argument as of 2026-07-10 (v2)
The FPO should not try to *replace* the middle of the chain — the RBI decomposition shows the classic middleman's trading margin is thin (~5%); the money sits in the retail+wastage block and in the credit spread. Instead it should **own the two things incumbents structurally can't offer — a forward order book (demand contract) and a trusted quality gate — while renting all physical assets**, using near-zero-marginal-cost AI advisory to shape member supply to that order book, and solving the *real* lock (day-0 cash + dry-season credit at 15–24%) via pledge/anchor finance rather than its own ₹3-lakh balance sheet. One semi-perishable crop, one district, Sahyadri's sequencing (demand first, assets later). Profitability comes from per-quintal linkage fees + quality arbitrage; subsidy is acceleration, never oxygen.

## Claims
### C1 — The prize is mis-located: not the trader's margin, but retail+wastage and the credit spread
- **Status:** ⚔️ contested → upgrading on RBI page-cites (Sonnet extraction in flight)
- **Evidence:** RBI TOP 2024 (farmer 33.5/36/37%, trader ~5.3%, wholesaler ~5.3%); CIPHET losses (tomato 12.44%); arthiya credit 15–24% vs KCC 7%
- **Strongest challenger:** Disintermediation school — "many stacked 5%s across 3–4 intermediaries still sum to a big wedge." Needs the full stage decomposition to resolve.
- **History:** v1 (07-09, PS2-era instinct imported): "middlemen capture the wedge — cut them out." → v2 (07-10): inverted by RBI data pull; the "middleman rent" is mostly downstream. *Trigger: Link Ledger session.*

### C2 — Working capital is the binding constraint and it selects the link
- **Status:** 🟢 settled (survived Adversary working-capital stress framing; no counter-evidence found)
- **Evidence:** IJEE 2022 — majority of FPOs hold <₹3 lakh working capital ≈ one truckload; day-0 farmer payment vs T+15/30 buyer receipts
- **History:** v1 (07-10): stated as "thin balance sheet" qualitative → v2 (07-10): quantified, elevated to *selector* of the recommendation. *Trigger: FPO study numbers.*

### C3 — "Own" means own the order book + quality gate; rent everything physical
- **Status:** ⚔️ contested — the brief's own Exhibit F ("moving up the chain works better") is the counter-argument; Sahyadri eventually DID buy assets
- **Evidence:** Sahyadri sequence (contracts → assets, 15 yrs); Dell/Li&Fung coordination logic; deletion table in Interrogation Step 2
- **Strongest challenger:** Move-up-the-chain school — value addition is where realized returns rose (brief, Exhibit F)
- **Resolution path:** reconcile via *sequencing*, not either/or: order-book-first IS the year-0–2 answer; assets are the year-3+ answer once volume de-risks them. Needs Sahyadri early-years financials (Ledger queue #6) to settle.
- **History:** v1 (07-10): "asset-light forever" → v2 (07-10): softened to sequencing after Sahyadri precedent pull showed assets were how they scaled margin. *Trigger: Anchor Buyer/Exemplar persona sourcing.*

### C4 — Advisory is a supply-shaping and retention weapon, not a revenue line; AI collapses its cost

- **Evidence:** agritech pivots away from paid advisory (to verify: DeHaat, Plantix); Exhibit D public data stack; LLM voice in dialect

### C5 — Unbundle the arthiya, don't eliminate him: match day-0 cash, undercut 15–24% credit, add what opacity can't copy (grading, traceability)
- **Status:** 🌱 hypothesis, thesis-shaping — candidate for THE one-sentence recommendation

- **History:** v1 (07-10): born from Arthiya persona sourcing. *Trigger: "his profit is the credit book, not the trade."*
