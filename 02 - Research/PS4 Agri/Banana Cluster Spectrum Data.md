## Constants across all clusters (spectrum axes must NOT differentiate on these)
- **Ripening charge ₹2.00/kg everywhere.**
- **Commission agents 85–90% of volume everywhere** (structure is national; the platform's target function is universal).
- Grade tiers similar (A ₹9–12 / B ₹7–9 / C ₹4–6; export ₹13–15); farmgate ₹9–10 agent channel / ₹10–11 FPO / ₹12–14 organized.

## Cluster-by-cluster detail (for panel drill-downs)

### GJ C1 — the volume giant
75–80% surplus exported to North India. 11 MIDH TC labs, demand 6.7 Cr plants/yr, saplings ₹12–17. Transport: Delhi ₹4/kg road, ₹2/kg Kisan Rail (post-subsidy); Rajasthan ₹2; Punjab ₹4.5; Haryana ₹3.5. Export: farm-gate carton packing → reefer → JNPT → ME 10–14 days @ ₹3/kg/km; Europe unviable (>30 days). Free APEDA packhouses for registered FPOs. Transit bruising 7% (open truck 5–10%).

### MH C1 (Jalgaon) — the GI proof + the loss cautionary tale
73% of MH output. Kisan Rail Jalgaon→Delhi 1,100 MT/wk. Delhi ₹4 road / ₹4.5 rail; Punjab ₹5. 25 TC labs; Jain >10 Cr plants/yr @ ₹13–17. **'Jalgaon Banana' GI realizes ₹15/kg export-quality vs ₹10 regular: the +50% GI premium precedent.** Grade A ₹10–11 / B ₹7–8 / C ₹4 / export ₹13–15. Only 2 ripening chambers (100 MT) for 2.61 Mn MT production. **30.5% total loss — retail 14.5, field 6.8, transit 4.0, ripening/storage 3.6.**

### AP C1 — the state-push cluster
Area doubled 2015–20 (CAGR 10%). State reefer trains Tadipatri→JNPT. Transport: TG/TN ₹1.5, MH ₹3, Delhi ₹6.5 road / ₹5.5 rail. 2 TC labs (50 L saplings) vs demand 4.9 Cr. 16 cold storages (97,500 MT) largely unused — bananas can't sit. 41 ripening chambers, 1,764 MT.

### AP C2 — the local-variety pocket
Production +11%/yr on TC adoption. 60% consumed in-state; Odisha ₹3, WB ₹5.25, KA ₹3.5, TG/TN ₹2.25. Zero TC labs. GI-tagged Chakrakeli/Karpura, unmarketed. Grade A ₹12 / B ₹9 / C ₹5–6 (local-variety premium visible). 19 chambers. Losses 23% (retail 9.4, storage 4.8, transit 1.6).

### TN C1 — the sibling cluster
50/50 G9 vs local (Virupakshi, Nendran). 60% out-of-state to Cochin/Bangalore/Mysore @ ₹1–1.5/kg. Kazhani FPO direct-exports G9 via Madurai Airport (~6 MT/wk). 2 TC labs (23 L, SPIC). Corrugated ₹45/kg vs wooden ₹42/kg. 24 chambers. **Losses 9%** (retail 3.9, transit 1–2 — short distances).

### TN C2 (Theni) — OUR CLUSTER
Stable 17k Ha, production CAGR 5.4% via high-density planting + drip fertigation. Kerala absorbs ~all surplus (Cochin ₹351 Cr trade — GT Deck). Cochin ₹1–1.5/kg, 3–6 h; sea ME 10–14 d @ ₹2–3/kg/km; air Malaysia/Maldives 24 h @ ₹2/kg/km. **Zero TC labs** (saplings from Hosur/Coimbatore — input-cost gap the platform can aggregate away). Corporates 3–4% only. **5 govt packhouses FREE to FPOs.** 5 ripening chambers, 895 MT. Varietal premium ladder on-cluster: G9 ₹10 → Red Banana ₹15–18 → Nendran ₹18–20. GI Virupakshi + Sirumalai active but zero brand recall.

### UP C1 & C2 — the stranded volume
C1: 55% of UP production; exports only overland to Nepal (Sonauli LCS); 1,700 km to JNPT = maritime unviable. Bihar <₹2–3, Delhi ₹3. Zero TC labs. C2: 19% land/21% production; 80% to Bihar; no exporters. Both: losses 22% (production 8–9, transit 9–10).

## The Theni verdict (feeds spectrum scoring — argument, not rigging)
1. **Efficiency floor:** 9% loss vs 22–30.5% everywhere north — the TN clusters start where others want to end up.
2. **Proximity moat:** ₹1–1.5/kg to a ₹351 Cr captive market (Kerala) + three export exits (Cochin/Tuticorin/Chennai) vs UP's 1,700-km stranding. Matches the corridor-length law (RBI 30.8% @ 1,100 km vs TNAU 52–65% @ 130 km — Ledger §B5).
3. **GI headroom:** Jalgaon proves GI = +50%/kg; Theni holds TWO GI tags at zero brand recall — the only cluster with proven-premium mechanics still unmonetized.
4. **Weakest incumbent competition:** no corporate anchor (3–4%), no Jain-style input monopoly — whitespace for platform entry.
5. **Precedent on-site:** BVC2 (65.45% farmer share) ran in **Chinnamanur, Theni district** — our model's proof point is inside our cluster.
6. Honest cons: smallest area of major clusters (17k Ha); zero TC labs (input dependency); ripening capacity thin (895 MT); commission 10% vs 4–6% north (higher extraction = bigger platform prize, but also entrenched agents).
