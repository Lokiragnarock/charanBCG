# Schools of Thought
> Each school steel-manned: its claim, its precedents, its kill-condition. We must be able to argue every one better than its believers — the one we reject well is element-4 material; the one we adopt gets stress-tested hardest.

## 1. The Disintermediation School — "cut out the middleman"
- **Claim:** intermediaries extract rent; direct farm-to-buyer connection returns it to farmers.
- **Believers/precedents:** eNAM's design, farmer-consumer markets (Rythu Bazar etc., brief Exhibit F), most agritech marketing decks, political economy of the 2020 farm laws.
- **Steel-man:** even thin per-layer margins stack across 3–4 layers; and the *option* to bypass disciplines everyone's pricing.
- **Kill-condition (largely met):** RBI TOP shows trader ~5.3% — the rent isn't where this school points. eNAM <1% of trade after 5+ years shows bypass-by-platform doesn't move behaviour.
- **Verdict trend:** ⚰️-leaning; survives only as the "option to bypass" pricing-discipline argument.

## 2. The Move-Up-the-Chain School — "value addition is the value"
- **Claim:** primary marketing is a commodity trap; returns live in grading, storage, processing, retail (the brief's own Exhibit F evidence).
- **Precedents:** Sahyadri (₹1,954.7 Cr FY25), MAHAGRAPES, HOPCOMS, Amul (the century-proof).
- **Steel-man:** every FPC that got rich did this; staying downstream-shallow is *why* 1,000+ FPOs underperform.
- **Kill-condition:** capital/capability math — median FPO (<₹3 lakh WC, 2 staff) attempting processing year-1 is a CRASH. Also survivorship bias: we see Sahyadri, not the FPCs that died buying cold storage.
- **Verdict trend:** right about the destination, wrong about the starting move → absorbed into C3 as *sequencing*.

## 3. The Coordination School — "own information and contracts, not assets"
- **Claim:** the FPO's unfair assets are trust, committed volume, and data; monetize via order books, quality gates, and rented logistics (Dell/Li & Fung logic).
- **Precedents:** Ninjacart (asset-light aggregation), ITC eChoupal (information changed farmgate prices without owning farms), early Sahyadri.
- **Steel-man:** highest margin per rupee of capital; matches the FPO's actual balance sheet.
- **Kill-condition:** if quality premia don't exist for the chosen crop (no buyer pays for grading), or if contracts can't hold against side-selling, coordination has nothing to sell. Also: asset-light players get squeezed once buyers can reach volume directly.
- **Verdict trend:** current favourite (C3, C5) — therefore gets the hardest Adversary pass.

## 4. The Institution School — "Amul took 30 years; trust is the technology"
- **Claim:** the binding constraint is organizational trust, not economics; patient capital + member discipline compound; subsidy is legitimate investment in a public good.
- **Precedents:** Amul/GCMMF, Mulkanoor cooperative, the 10,000-FPO scheme's own logic.
- **Steel-man:** every fast agri-play that ignored institution-building died; side-selling is a trust problem before it's a price problem.
- **Kill-condition (for us):** the case explicitly demands unit-economics viability, "not impact at the client's expense" — a 30-year trust argument doesn't answer an 8-day case brief. But its side-selling insight survives inside C5.
- **Verdict trend:** rejected as *frame*, harvested for *mechanisms* (member equity, transparent price formula, women's account payment).

## 5. The Credit-First School — "the chain runs on credit, whoever lends, owns"
- **Claim:** the arthiya's power is the loan book, not the trade; displace the credit and the linkage follows for free.
- **Precedents:** interlinked-transactions literature (IGC, Punjab studies); KCC's failure to displace informal credit despite a 20-point rate advantage (proximity/flexibility beats price).
- **Steel-man:** ≥35% of farm credit via agents at 15–24%; Lakshmi's cash-before-Friday constraint decides where produce goes.
- **Kill-condition:** FPO-as-lender = NBFC regulation + default risk on a ₹3-lakh balance sheet (CRASH). KCC's failure warns that *rate* alone doesn't win — the product is proximity + flexibility.
- **Verdict trend:** right diagnosis, dangerous prescription → absorbed into C5 as *partner rails* (pledge/e-NWR/anchor advances), not own-book lending.
