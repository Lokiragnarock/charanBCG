# Precedents — the case law
> Treated like law: a precedent binds only within its facts. Every entry states what it proves, what it does NOT prove, and what we still need to extract. Numbers → [[../PS4 - Link Ledger|Ledger]] rows.

| Precedent | Facts | Proves | Does NOT prove | To extract |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sahyadri Farms** (Nashik, 2010–) | ₹1,954.7 Cr FY25, 26% CAGR, 18–30k farmers; grape-export origin; assets after order book (ICRA, MANAGE, RBI-CAB) | Demand-first sequencing scales; farmer-owned governance survives scale | That it's replicable on a median FPO's balance sheet, or outside export-grade horticulture | Early-years (2010–14) unit economics from MANAGE/RBI-CAB PDFs — queue #6 |
| **MAHAGRAPES** (1991–) | Cooperative-federation grape exporter | Federation model can hold quality standards across many co-ops | Domestic-market economics (it's an export story) | Fee structure between federation and member co-ops |
| **HOPCOMS** (Karnataka) | State-backed horti marketing, own retail outlets | Farmer-to-retail can run for decades | Commercial viability without state scaffolding | Their procurement-price vs mandi-price spread |
| **Amul / GCMMF** | Dairy, daily cash payment, village societies | Daily liquidity + transparent testing = side-selling solved; trust compounds | Transfers to F&V (milk is daily-flow, gradeable in seconds, monopsony-friendly) — the disanalogy IS the lesson | The exact payment/testing mechanism as design template for our quality gate |
| **ITC eChoupal** (2000s) | Kiosk price information at village level | **Asset-light coordination beats ownership**: ITC never owned the physical chain, it re-coordinated an existing ecosystem — now the operating template for [[e-Choupal Banana OS]] (07-17 pivot) | A member-owned org can capture the gain (ITC, the buyer, captured it) — the FPO-capture question is OUR delta over the precedent | Documented price-uplift magnitude; which sanchalak/samyojak functions map to contractor functions in banana |
| **Mango PHC system** (RBI WPS 06/2024 pp.32–34) | Pre-harvest contractors fix price+tonnage off flowering, fund orchard upkeep, aggregate; farmer share 42–43% — highest of the three fruits | A *bundled* contractor coexists with the best farmer share on record in fruits; contractor = price insurance + credit + aggregation, not a toll | That banana contractors offer the same bundle (verify in Theni) | Whether Theni banana contractors pre-finance against standing crop the way PHCs do |
| **eNAM** (2016–) | National e-market; <1% of trade by 2021 (brief, Exhibit C) | Platform-bypass without solving logistics/credit/trust moves nothing | That digital price discovery is worthless — it's necessary, not sufficient | — (already a Ledger row) |
| **Contract farming** (Kumar 2016, brief Exhibit F) | ~+81% net income vs independent | Assured-offtake + advisory bundles lift income massively | Enforceability at smallholder scale; who bears rejection risk | The crop/context of the +81% (verify before leaning on it) |
| **KCC / interest subvention** | 7% (eff. ~4%) formal credit, yet ≥35% of credit still via agents at 15–24% | **Rate doesn't beat proximity + flexibility** — the strongest warning for any "cheaper credit" pitch | That informal credit is undisplaceable — pledge/e-NWR rails unexplored at scale | e-NWR pledge-finance volumes and rates — queue #5 |
| **Agritech cohort** (Ninjacart, DeHaat, Plantix…) | VC-funded; repeated pivots from advisory → transactions/inputs | Advisory alone doesn't monetize (C4); asset-light aggregation can work with outside capital | FPO-transferability (VC subsidized their losses for years) | One named pivot with dates/numbers for C4's citation |
