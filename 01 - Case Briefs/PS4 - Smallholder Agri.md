# PS4 — Smallholder Agri: Owning the Market Linkage

> **ACTIVE CASE (pivot from PS2)** — see [[../04 - Decision Log/2026-07-10 - Pivot to PS4|decision log]].

## The task (one paragraph)
You advise a **Farmer Producer Organization (FPO)** that wants to lift member incomes by **owning the link between farm and market** via an **advisory-plus-linkage** model (tell farmers what/when to grow → aggregate → sell closer to the end buyer). The decision: **which link (or links) of the value chain should the FPO own FIRST** — the one that lifts farmer income most *while the FPO can actually resource it and the economics close*.

## The value chain (pick from this)
input supply → crop advisory → aggregation → grading & storage → transport & logistics → processing / value addition → direct sale (buyer / retailer / consumer)

## What a defensible answer must contain (from the brief, verbatim intent)
1. **Where, concretely** — the crop and context that make the chosen link right (perishable vs grain give different answers)
2. **What owning it takes** — capability, capital, risk the FPO must absorb; can it realistically?
3. **The math closes for BOTH sides** — farmer income rises AND FPO unit economics viable. "Not impact at the client's expense, nor margin at the farmer's."
4. Plus the framing question: **profitable business or subsidy-dependent impact play?**

## The tension (the case's own kill-switch)
- Intermediaries do **real work**: aggregation, transport, **credit**, price risk, sorting, last-mile reach. Their margin is not pure rent.
- Taking the linkage = taking those costs and risks, on tiny volumes, thin balance sheet, limited management.
- Evidence cited: FPOs stuck at "crop planning + inputs + primary marketing" **underperform**; the winners moved **up the chain** (direct retail, value-add, storage, processing). But that's exactly where capital/capability/risk bite hardest.
- The crops with the biggest intermediary wedge (**perishables, 50–70% intermediary share**) are the hardest/riskiest to handle.
