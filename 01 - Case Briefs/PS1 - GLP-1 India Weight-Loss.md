# PS1 — GLP-1: The India Weight-Loss Opportunity

## The situation (one paragraph)
We advise a **major Indian generics pharma company** (Sun / Dr. Reddy's / Cipla-type) planning its GLP-1 play as **semaglutide goes off-patent in India in 2026** and a generic wave begins. India is out-of-pocket and price-sensitive, so the molecule commoditises fast — **the money and the moat will not stay in the molecule**.

## The central tension
> Post-patent, semaglutide becomes a cheap commodity (net prices falling even before generics). Competing on price/access alone in an out-of-pocket market is a race to the bottom — despite the market's enormous size.

**The case's own thesis: GLP-1 is a behaviour business, not just a molecule.**

## Key numbers (all from the case PDF — external figures still need our own citations)

### US as leading indicator
- ~1 in 4 US adults using / has used / considering a GLP-1
- Active base: ~6M (2022) → ~16M (today) → projected 30–35M by 2030
- Users cut calories ~29%; **food habits revert within 1–3 months post-lapse**
- But **20–40% of wellness behaviours persist** post-lapse (supplements, fitness, tracking, personal care)
- ~34% lapse; ~60% of lapsed plan to restart; 12%+ cycle on/off → **adherence is a flywheel, not a funnel** (Exhibit B)
- ~80% of US adults still haven't tried one → huge headroom

### India's metabolic burden (ICMR-INDIAB 2023, NFHS-5 2019-21 — verify originals)
- **101M diabetics + 136M prediabetics**
- **254M generalised obesity; 351M abdominal obesity**
- Overweight/obesity ~24% of women, ~23% of men, rising across income groups & younger ages
- Open question: how much converts to a *paying, out-of-pocket* market, and how fast

### Exhibit A — four purchase archetypes (active users; each a distinct GTM)
| Archetype | Share | Buys around the drug |
|---|---|---|
| Lifestyle optimization | 41% | fitness, wellness routines, habit-building |
| Disease management | 27% | condition + GLP side-effect management |
| Aesthetic transformation | 19% | medical aesthetics, beauty |
| All-in on GLP-1s | 13% | whole health & wellness life transformation |

### Exhibit C — India's weight-loss ambition (directional, low N=78)
India skews moderate: 26% target 10–14% body weight, 28% target <10% — but a notable 24% target >25% (ambitious tail similar to US/UK).

### Exhibit D — molecule commoditising
- US price now ~$250–400/mo (oral $150–300, injectable $300–550)
- **US consumer ideal price: $50–90/mo**
- Oral bioavailability ~1% vs ~89% injectable → oral needs **146x the API** → API scale matters (India strength!)

### Exhibit E — spend reallocation
Food-spend savings split ~⅓ savings / ⅓ GLP co-pay / ⅓ other (supplements, beauty, apparel, fitness, aesthetics). ~70% of GLP households changed eating behaviour → demand footprint ~+50% beyond active users (16M users ≈ 24M in demand).

## The decision we must make
1. **Where to play** — which segments first, given India's ambition levels + out-of-pocket reality
2. **How to win beyond the molecule** — full-stack offer: app, coaching, adherence engine, wellness/aesthetics ecosystem, services
3. **Pricing & access** — price for out-of-pocket; lead vs defend against the generic wave
4. **5-year shape** — directional patient reach, revenue, margin trajectory (not a full model)

## First-read instincts (to stress-test, not to keep)
- India's out-of-pocket + $50–90 ideal price + generics = molecule margin gone in ~18 months → the durable business is **adherence + the behaviour stack**, monetised per-member per-month, not per-pill.
- The 146x API fact is a structural gift to an Indian generics major: **oral semaglutide at scale is an India-shaped moat** (API capacity + cost).
- Archetypes map differently in India: "disease management" (diabetes-first, doctor-mediated) is likely the beachhead, not US-style lifestyle/aesthetic — but the aspiration data (Exhibit C) says don't ignore the ambitious tail.
- Lapse-and-restart flywheel means CLV math beats funnel math → retention infrastructure is the asset.
