# The Member Farmer — "Lakshmi" 👩‍🌾
> Case-user persona, built from national survey data, not vibes. The brief planted the women datapoint in the decision section (~43% of agri labour, +20–30% yield if input gap closed — FAO, brief Exhibit E) — she is why.

**⚡ The number that reframes everything: farming is only ~37% of her household income.** She is income-diversified because farming alone doesn't clear subsistence. An FPO pitch that treats her as "a farmer" misses that she's a household CFO allocating labour across wages, livestock, and crop — the FPO competes for her *time and trust*, not just her produce.

## Her decision function on harvest day (simulator input)
`sell_to = argmax( price_today × trust_it_gets_paid − transport_cost − hassle )` subject to **cash-before-Friday ≥ everything**.
Side-selling is rational liquidity management, not betrayal — the arthiya pays today and lends in the dry season ([the "farmer's bank" role — IGC WP 2013](https://www.theigc.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Haq-Et-Al-2013-Working-Paper.pdf)). Any retention mechanism must beat this equation.

## What she needs from each link
| Link | Requirement (evidence-backed) |
|---|---|
| Advisory | Voice, her dialect, timed to crop stage — not a dashboard |
| Aggregation | Collection point within cart distance (mandi density says the State won't provide it); honest weighing |
| Payment | Day-0/1, UPI **to her account** — intra-household income control is the +20–30% lever's mechanism |
| Credit | Input credit against committed acreage at <15% — undercut the arthiya's 15–24% while staying viable |
