# The Anchor Buyer — "the procurement head" 🏬 & The Exemplar — Sahyadri 🍇

## Part 1 — The Anchor Buyer (who our order book serves)
Organized buyer (retail chain DC, processor, exporter, HORECA aggregator). What he pays for is not produce — it's **predictability**: volume on schedule, grade consistency, food-safety traceability, GST invoice, one counterparty instead of 400.
- Pays T+15/T+30 — which is exactly why the FPO needs pledge/anchor finance to bridge to the farmer's day-0 (Interrogation Q9/Q10)
- Rejects lots at the dock; rejection risk lands on whoever owns the produce at that moment — a contract-design question, not a logistics one
- Will pay a premium for grade+traceability only if switching cost from his current mandi supplier is covered
> Buyer persona numbers (procurement premiums, rejection rates) = 🟡 gap in [[../02 - Research/PS4 - Link Ledger|Link Ledger]] queue #4.

## Part 2 — The Exemplar: Sahyadri Farms, Nashik (the case's own Exhibit F name, quantified)
| Fact | Number | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue FY25 | **₹1,954.7 crore**, ~26% CAGR since 2010 | [ICRA rating rationale](https://www.icra.in/Rating/GetRationalReportFilePdf?id=126408) / [global-agriculture.com](https://www.global-agriculture.com/india-region/sahyadri-farms-a-farmer-led-collective-transforming-indian-agriculture/) |
| Scale path | ₹319.8 Cr (FY18) → ₹997.2 Cr (FY23) | ICRA |
| Farmers | 18,000+ registered → ~30,000 small/marginal, 100% farmer-owned equity, equal votes | [Sahyadri FPC](https://www.sahyadrifpc.com/about-us) / FMO |
| Volume | ~1.24 lakh tonnes horticulture produce (2020-21) | [MANAGE case study](https://www.manage.gov.in/publications/reports/Sahyadri-report.pdf) |
| Infrastructure sequence | collection centres → cold chain + reefer fleet → processing → 5–7 retail stores (Mumbai/Pune/Nashik) — **assets came AFTER the order book** | [FMO project detail](https://www.fmo.nl/project-detail/56346) |
| Origin | started with ONE crop (export grape), ONE geography (Nashik) | [RBI CAB success-story study](https://cab.rbi.org.in/docs/Special_Study/Sahyadri%20Farmers%20Producer%20Company%20Limited%20-%20a%20success%20story.pdf) |

**⚡ Why the Exemplar matters to OUR rec:** Sahyadri validates the emerging thesis sequence — single crop, single district, demand-contract first, assets later — and gives us a cited "it works at ₹2,000 Cr scale" slide. It also sets the Adversary trap: Sahyadri took 15 years; our rec must state what the FIRST 24 months look like, not cosplay the endgame.
