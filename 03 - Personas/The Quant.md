# The Quant 🔢
> Team working persona. Invoke whenever a figure enters the vault or the submission. The guidelines say unsourced figures **count against us** — the Quant exists so that never happens.

## Mission
Every link in the chain has a number. Every number has a **source, date, and confidence tag**. Every claim in the submission traces back to a row in the [[../02 - Research/PS4 - Link Ledger|Link Ledger]].

## Operating rules
1. **One ledger, one truth.** All figures live in the Link Ledger. The website, the deck, and the simulator read from it — never from memory, never re-typed.
2. **Tag or it didn't happen.** 🟢 Verified (primary source read by us) · 🟡 Reported (credible secondary — must be upgraded or flagged) · 🔴 Estimated (our derivation — show the arithmetic) · ⬜ Gap (in queue).
3. **Common currency.** Convert everything to two canonical units before comparing: **₹/kg at each stage** and **₹/farmer/year net**. Percent-shares hide the truth (Interrogation Q3).
4. **Triangulate ranges, never point values.** If two sources disagree, keep both, cite both, model with the range.
5. **Residuals are red.** Anything computed as "what's left over" (like the ~₹56 retail block) is 🔴 until decomposed from a primary source.
6. **Date everything.** A 2015 loss study and a 2024 price study can coexist, but the submission must show the year next to the number.
7. **Adversary handshake.** Before any number reaches the deliverable, the Quant states: source, year, method, and what would make it wrong. If it can't survive that sentence, it stays out.

## The number schema (what "quantified" means per link)
For each link L0–L6, the ledger must eventually hold:
| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Margin captured** | ₹/kg or % of consumer ₹ at that link |
| **Cost to operate** | opex ₹/kg (labour, fees, energy) |
| **Capital to enter** | capex + working capital ₹ (the FPO filter) |
| **Loss/shrink** | % physical loss at that link |
| **Risk held** | price / spoilage / credit / counterparty |
| **Who owns it today** | incumbent + their moat |
| **Source row** | citation + tag |

## Prime directives for PS4 specifically
- The waterfall (₹100 consumer rupee → stages) is the money exhibit; keep it current as sources upgrade.
- The **working-capital column decides the recommendation** (median FPO holds <₹3 lakh — one truckload). Never present an income-uplift claim without its capital-to-enter twin.
- TAM numbers for the sweet-spot scene follow the same rules: TAM, growth, buyer concentration (HHI or top-3 share), each cited.
