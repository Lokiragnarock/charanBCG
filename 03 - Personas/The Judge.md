# The Judge 🧑‍⚖️
> The reader persona. Every deliverable decision is tested against this person, not against our own taste.

## Who they are
A BCG consultant/principal skimming dozens of R1 submissions late at night. 5–10 minutes per entry unless something earns more. Has read the brief they wrote; knows its traps (they planted them). Allergic to: unsourced figures, optionality ("we could do A or B or C"), impact hand-waving, AI-generated genericness.

## What earns their next 10 minutes (from guidelines + brief, in their words)
1. **One clear, specific call** — a link, a crop, a geography, named.
2. **Numbers with citations they can verify** — the guidelines explicitly say they will go back and check.
3. **Trap awareness** — the brief pre-refutes naive aggregation (1,000+ FPOs underperform at it); did the team notice?
4. **Both sides of the math** — farmer income up AND FPO viable. They wrote "not impact at the client's expense, nor margin at the farmer's" deliberately.
5. **Evidence of directing the AI** — first answer → pushback → what changed. They are scoring the *collaboration*, not the polish.
6. **The women datapoint** — they placed ~43% / +20–30% in the decision section, not the exhibits. It's a test of whether teams treat it as decoration or design input.

## Judge-proofing checklist (run before shipping anything)
- [ ] Can the judge state our recommendation in one sentence after 30 seconds?
- [ ] Is every figure clickable to a source with a year?
- [ ] Does the site work on a locked-down corporate laptop (no WebGL, no sound)?
- [ ] Is there a sentence no other team could write?
- [ ] Did we show a rejected idea they'd have expected us to choose?
- [ ] Does the simulator's "our recommendation" preset load in <5 seconds of curiosity?
