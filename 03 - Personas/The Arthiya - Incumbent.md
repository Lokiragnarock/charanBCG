# The Arthiya — "Sethji" 🏦
> Incumbent persona. Know the enemy — and notice he's not who the narrative says he is. Primary sources: [IGC working paper "Who is the arthi"](https://www.theigc.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Haq-Et-Al-2013-Working-Paper.pdf), [Kapur & Krishnamurthy, "Understanding Mandis" (CASI UPenn)](https://casi.sas.upenn.edu/sites/default/files/research/Understanding%20Mandis%20-%20D.%20Kapur,%20M.%20Krishnamurthy.pdf), [Scroll.in on arthiyas](https://scroll.in/article/980537/middlemen-or-service-providers-who-are-the-arthiyas-who-are-a-key-part-of-farming-in-punjab).

## What he actually does (the service bundle)
| Function | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Trade facilitation | Licensed by the market committee: display, cleaning, sorting, auction, payment | CASI UPenn |
| **Banking** | "The farmer's bank" — dry-season consumption loans + input credit, zero paperwork, instant | IGC WP 2013 |
| Credit share | ≥35% of farm credit flows through agents | issingh / official estimates |
| His price | 15–24%+ interest vs institutional 8–10%; commission 2–4% farmer-side in practice (official: 2% buyer-side) | Interlinked Credit Markets (Punjab); CASI |
| The lock | **Interlinked transactions**: credit is tied to the obligation to sell through him — the stronger party captures the surplus | [Ideas for India on interlinkages](https://www.ideasforindia.in/topics/agriculture/addressing-the-economic-trade-offs-of-interlinkages-in-contemporary-agrarian-markets.html) |

## Why he wins (honest assessment — feeds Adversary attack #1)
- **Speed:** cash today, no forms, no KYC, no board meeting
- **Flexibility:** lends for weddings and hospitalizations, not just inputs — no bank or FPO does this
- **Information:** knows every farmer's acreage, character, and crop condition — a credit-scoring model built over generations
- **Staying power:** can cut margins for a season to starve a challenger; relationships survive one bad FPO year

## Where he's genuinely weak (the openings)
- His capital costs him more than institutional finance costs an FPO with NABARD backing — his 15–24% has a cost floor
- The interlink is resented — farmers describe it as exploitation when surveyed (Scroll.in, Punjab reporting)
- He doesn't do **quality**: no grading uplift, no traceability, no premium-market access — his model monetizes opacity, so transparency is a weapon he can't copy without self-harm
- RBI TOP data shows his *trading* margin is thin (~5%); his real profit is the credit book — attack the credit, not the trade

## Strategic conclusion for the rec
Don't frame as "eliminate the arthiya" — the RBI decomposition shows that's a ₹5 target. Frame as **unbundle him**: match his day-0 cash (pledge/anchor finance), undercut his credit (KCC-linked at <15%), and add what he structurally can't (grading, traceability, demand contracts). The FPO becomes the better bank-plus-market, not a cheaper trader.
